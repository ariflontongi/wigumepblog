The Million Dollar Barbie Dream: The Ruth Handler Story

What is Ruth Handler’s Net Worth and Salary?

Ruth Handler, the co-founder of Mattel Inc. and creator of the iconic Barbie doll, had an estimated net worth of $350 million at the time of her death in 2002. Her wealth was primarily attributed to her success in the toy industry and her numerous patents throughout her career. It is estimated that Handler made $12 million in revenue from the sale of the first Barbie doll in 1959.

Early Life and Education

Ruth Mosko was born in Denver, Colorado, in 1916. She was the youngest of ten children and grew up in a struggling family during the Great Depression. Despite her family’s financial struggles, Ruth was determined to succeed and worked hard to earn a scholarship to study at the University of Denver. After earning a degree in economics, she moved to Los Angeles with her husband, Elliot Handler, to start a new life.

Career

Handler’s career in the toy industry began when she co-founded Mattel in 1945 with her husband and their friend Harold Matson. The company initially produced picture frames, but Ruth had her sights set on creating toys. In 1955, Mattel produced the first “Ponytail” Barbie doll, named after Handler’s daughter Barbara. The doll quickly became a best-seller and paved the way for the Barbie franchise, including Barbie’s boyfriend, Ken, and later siblings, friends, and accessories.

Despite the initial success of Barbie, Mattel faced challenges in the 1970s, including litigation over alleged copyright infringement and criticism of the doll’s unrealistic body proportions. Handler stepped down as CEO of Mattel in 1973 but continued to serve on the board of directors until 1975.

Personal Life

Handler’s personal life was filled with tragedy and controversy. She and her husband had two children, Barbara and Ken, and later adopted a third child, Cheryl. In the 1960s, Handler was diagnosed with breast cancer and underwent a mastectomy. In the 1970s, she faced scrutiny for her involvement in a shady stock trading scheme and later went to jail for her role in the controversy.

Real Estate

In addition to her success in the toy industry, Handler also had a passion for real estate. She and her husband built a lavish home in Holmby Hills, California, that served as a hub for Hollywood’s elite. The estate was later sold to Playboy founder Hugh Hefner in 1971 and became the Playboy Mansion.

Legacy

Ruth Handler’s legacy is that of a pioneer and trailblazer in the toy industry. She paved the way for women in business and challenged gender norms with the creation of Barbie. However, her legacy is also fraught with controversy, including criticism of the unrealistic body image portrayed by Barbie and her involvement in the stock trading scandal.

How did Ruth Handler come up with the idea for the Barbie doll?

Ruth Handler invented the Barbie doll after noticing her daughter Barbara playing with paper dolls and wishing she had a more grown-up doll to play with. Handler saw a gap in the market for a doll that represented a more mature and fashionable woman, and thus the idea for Barbie was born.

What kind of impact did the Barbie doll have on society?

The Barbie doll had a significant impact on society, particularly in terms of gender roles and body image. Many critics argue that Barbie perpetuates unrealistic beauty standards and contributes to the objectification of women. What other toys did Ruth Handler invent?

In addition to Barbie, Ruth Handler also invented the Hot Wheels toy cars in the late 1960s, which quickly became a best-seller. She also had numerous patents for other toys, including a musical toothbrush and a doll that could walk and talk.
