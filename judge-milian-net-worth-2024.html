Early Life and Education

Marilyn Milian was born in Astoria, New York, to Cuban parents. She was raised in Miami, where she attended St. Brendan High School. Legal Career

Milian began her legal career as an assistant state attorney for the Dade County State Attorney’s Office. She later served in the Miami Circuit Court, where she spent five years in the Criminal Division. In 1999, she was appointed to the Miami-Dade County Court by then-Governor Jeb Bush.

The People’s Court

In 2001, Milian replaced Judge Jerry Sheindlin as the presiding judge on “The People’s Court,” becoming the first Latina judge on any English-language television court show. Her vibrant personality and sharp legal acumen quickly made her a favorite among viewers.
