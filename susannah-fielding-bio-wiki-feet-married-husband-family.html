Susannah Fielding Bio, facts

Susannah Fielding, also known as Susannah Glanville-Hearson, was born on the 10th day of June 1985; in Poole, Dorset, England. The British actress grew up in Portsmouth and, like James D’Arcy, attended school at Christ’s Hospital (Bluecoat School) in Sussex.

While Susannah Fielding was a student at Christ’s Hospital School, she discovered her talent for acting and later moved to London to train at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama; from which she graduated in 2006.

During her time at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama, she played lead roles as Trinculo in Patsy Rodenburg’s ‘The Tempest’ and Myrrah in Christian Burgess’ ‘Tales from Ovid’.

The world has been Susannah’s oyster since her graduation from the Guildhall School of Music and Drama. Susannah didn’t have to wait long for big acting gigs. From 2007 to 2008 she was a regular figure in the National Theater for her roles in major productions like Rosa in “The Rose Tattoo” (2007), Polya in “Philistines” (2007), Hero in “Much Ado About Nothing” and Together in “The Hour We Knew Nothing About Each Other” (2008).

Susannah would have her return to the National Theater in 2015 with her role as Mrs. Sullen in the production of ‘The Beaux Stratagem’. Other theatrical titles to his name include: ‘An Enemy of the People’ (2010) at the Sheffield Crucible Theatre, Sheffield; “The Merchant of Venice” (2011), in which she played the role of Portia at the Royal Shakespeare Theatre.
