Early Life and Career Beginnings

Erin Andrews was born in Lewiston, Maine, and raised in Tampa, Florida. She developed an interest in sports at a young age, thanks to her father, a six-time Emmy Award-winning journalist. After graduating from the University of Florida with a degree in Telecommunication, Andrews began her career as a freelance reporter for Fox Sports Florida in 2000.

Rise to Fame

Andrews’ big break came in 2004 when she joined ESPN as a sideline reporter for ESPN National Hockey Night. Her role expanded over the years to include reporting for College Football Saturday Primetime and Major League Baseball. Her charismatic personality and in-depth knowledge of sports quickly made her a fan favorite.

Transition to Fox Sports

In 2012, Andrews left ESPN to join Fox Sports. At Fox, she served as the lead sideline reporter for the network’s NFL broadcasting team and contributed to coverage of other major sporting events, including the World Series and the Super Bowl.

Dancing with the Stars

In addition to her sports broadcasting career, Andrews has also made a name for herself in the world of reality TV. She competed on the tenth season of Dancing with the Stars in 2010, finishing in third place. In 2014, she returned to the show as a co-host, a role she held until 2020.

Endorsements and Other Ventures

Andrews’ fame has also led to endorsement deals with various companies, including Reebok, Degree Deodorant, and Florida Orange Juice. She also launched her own line of sportswear, WEAR by Erin Andrews, in 2019.

Legal Battles and Settlements

In 2008, Andrews was the victim of a stalking incident that led to a lengthy legal battle. She eventually won a $55 million settlement, a portion of which she donated to organizations supporting victims of stalking and cybercrime.

Personal Life

Andrews married former NHL player Jarret Stoll in 2017. She has been open about her battles with cervical cancer and has used her platform to raise awareness about the disease.

Erin Andrews’ Net Worth 2024: A Projection
