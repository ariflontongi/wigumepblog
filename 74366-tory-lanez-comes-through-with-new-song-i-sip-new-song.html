Tory Lanez Comes Through With New Song &quot;I Sip&quot;

Tory Lanez has been steady dropping singles for a minute now, from the aggressive "Shooters" to the Future collaboration "Real Thing" to the intimate sex-jam "Skrrt, Skrrt," while fans patiently wait for him to drop his new Memories Don't Die album. And while the wait is admittedly on the longer side, Tory is a merciful creator, and has thankfully deemed us worthy of some new music in the interim. Today, the Torontonian serves up "I Sip," and ode to the liquor that would make the late Jim Lahey proud. The track has a relatively subdued vibe, and Tory lets his vocal chops shine as he waxes poetic about his addiction to a self-destructive lifestyle. 

"I sip way too much drank," sings Tory, "I don't think I can think, I won't try to tell lies, this blunt got me so high." He wants to stop, but he can't, or so he says. That doesn't stop him from spitting game on "I Sip," however, and if you've been waiting for some new music from Lanez, then look no further.
