Tennis Star Aryna Sabalenka's Partner Konstantin Koltsov Commits Suicide

Former NHL player Konstantin Koltsov, the partner of tennis star Aryna Sabalenka, passed away in what authorities are investigating as an “apparent suicide,” according to reports from Miami-Dade Police.

The heartbreaking incident unfolded at the St. Regis Bal Harbour Resort in the early hours of Monday, prompting a response from law enforcement. The Miami-Dade Police Homicide Bureau has taken charge of the investigation, emphasizing that no foul play is suspected.

The news sent shockwaves through the sports community, particularly in Belarus, where the Belarusian Hockey Federation expressed profound condolences to Koltsov‘s family, friends, and colleagues.

Konstantin Koltsov, known for his remarkable career in ice hockey, notably played for the Pittsburgh Penguins in the NHL, leaving a lasting impact on the sport. His dedication and talent were evident as he represented Belarus in two Olympic Games and nine world championships.

His relationship with Aryna Sabalenka, a celebrated tennis player, brought them into the public eye. Sabalenka was scheduled to compete in the upcoming Miami Open. However, the tragic turn of events has left her tennis future uncertain as she grapples with this devastating loss.

Beyond the sporting arena, Koltsov‘s passing has left a profound void in his personal life. He leaves behind three children from his previous marriage. His journey was marked by triumphs and challenges, including his divorce in 2020 before finding love again with Sabalenka.

The Pittsburgh Penguins and the Belarusian Hockey Federation extended their heartfelt condolences, reflecting on Koltsov‘s legacy as a talented athlete and beloved figure in the sports world.
